Quick and easy dinner ideas for hectic week nights!

The most important thing for the majority of people after a long day at work, college or doing the housework is to want to get home. Most people want to go home after a long day at work, school, or doing household chores, and the last thing they want to do is sit in the kitchen for hours. Anyone can experience some late night cravings, a lack of energy and a need for food. The good news, however, is that it's not difficult to make a healthy and delicious meal.

One of the busiest weeks that I can remember was when I was doing a lot of things and couldn't find time to cook. I began cooking at home for the first time, using foods I could find in my kitchen. It was a great time-saver, money-saver and surprisingly one of my favourite meals at the time.

In this article I will include some easy dinner ideas for busy week night dinners that are easy to make, yet not expensive, and good for the whole family!

Giving up your time for a quality meal is both easy and beneficial. It's easy and good to sacrifice time for a great meal.

Even with limited cooking time (20-30 minutes), there are many advantages of cooking at home.

Some advantages include:

You're aware of the ingredients in what you eat.

Cooked at home, meals are usually healthier than those at restaurants.

You can save money.

It allows you to modify spices and ingredients, as you prefer.

Leftover food items can be eaten at lunch the following day.

Cooking at night can be a lot less stressful if you plan it out a bit beforehand.


1. Vegetable Fried Rice



Fried rice with vegetables is one of the easiest-to-make foods.

Ingredients

Cooked rice

Carrot

Beans

Capsicum

Onion

Garlic

Soy sauce

Black pepper

Oil

Method

Add a small amount of oil, sauté garlic and onions, add chopped veggies and then stir in the cooked rice. Season with soy sauce, pepper and salt (as necessary). Combine all ingredients and mix for several minutes.

Served hot with curd or simple cucumber salad.

Paneer is already cooked when served with roti.


Paneer Bhurji  recipe is very low in time and high in protein



Ingredients

Crumbled paneer

Onion

Tomato

Green chilli

Turmeric

Red chilli powder

Garam masala

Coriander leaves

Add spices to onions and tomatoes before adding paneer. Mix well and garnish with fresh coriander.

Serve with chapati, roti or bread.


3. Masala Omelette Sandwich



It's a great time-saving recipe.

Crack eggs and mix with chopped onions, tomatoes, green chillies, coriander and salt. Make an omelette and put between two slices of toasted bread.

May also add cheese or cucumber for additional flavor.


4. One-Pot Vegetable Khichdi



Khichdi is easy to eat, healthy and comforting.

Add rice, moong dal, chopped vegetables, turmeric, cumin and water to a pressure cooker. Cook until soft.

Enjoy with curd, pickle or papad for a wholesome meal.


5. Mixed Vegetable Pasta



Pasta can be a healthy food, as well as a delicious one!

Cook pasta, blanch vegetables, such as capsicum, corn, broccoli, carrot and mushrooms. Stir in the tomato puree, herbs and cooked pasta.

If desired, stir in a bit of cheese.


6. Chickpea Salad Bowl



This is a great recipe for those that like something a bit lighter.

Mix boiled chickpeas with cucumber, tomato, onion, carrot, sweet corn, lemon juice, black pepper and coriander.

It's vibrant, invigorating and filling, too.


7. Mixed Vegetable Upma



Upma doesn't stop at breakfast. It's also a great dinner!

Roast the semolina and add onions, mustard seeds, curry leaves, carrots, peas and beans to cook.

Serve with coconut chutney or pickle.


8. Dal Tadka Jeera rice



The most popular comfort food, dal and rice, continue to be popular in India.

Cook some simple dal with basic spices, onions, tomatoes and garlic in yellow. Serve with aromatic Jeera rice.

A healthy, filling (but not weighty) meal.

Savour a relaxing evening without having to sacrifice valuable time.

When you have some of your food ready to go, it will be a lot easier to cook.

These are some helpful hints:

Cut vegetables and keep in air-tight containers.

Make ginger-garlic paste for a week.

Fry one cup of rice, to be used for fried rice the next day.

Keep boiled potatoes, chickpeas or paneer in refrigerator.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the fastest cookery recipe?

Usually it takes about 20 minutes to make vegetable fried rice, masala omelette sandwich and paneer bhurji.

Is it inexpensive to have healthy dinners?

Yes. There are some healthy and affordable ingredients such as rice, dal, vegetables, eggs, seasonal fruits etc.


What can I do to cook quicker?

Planning and prepping ingredients at home, and having an organised pantry, will save a lot of cooking time!

If I'm really pressed for time, what should I cook?

Simple food items such as upma, khichdi, sandwiches, salads or scrambled paneer are great options.
